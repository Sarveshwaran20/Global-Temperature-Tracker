Global Warming Awareness

What is Global Warming?

Global warming refers to the long-term increase in Earth's average surface temperature due to human activities, primarily the burning of fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and gas. This process releases greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide (CO2) and methane (CH4) into the atmosphere, trapping heat and disrupting the planet's natural climate systems.

The consequences of global warming include rising sea levels, more frequent and severe weather events, loss of biodiversity, and disruptions to ecosystems and human societies. Addressing global warming requires global cooperation, innovation, and a commitment to reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

Did You Know?

The last decade was the hottest in recorded history, and the Arctic is warming twice as fast as the rest of the planet!

Did You Know?

  • The Earth's average temperature has risen by 1.1°C since the late 19th century.
  • Carbon dioxide levels in the atmosphere are at their highest in 2 million years.
  • Sea levels have risen by about 8 inches (20 cm) since 1880, and the rate of rise is accelerating.
  • Global warming is causing the Arctic ice to shrink by 13% per decade.
  • Renewable energy sources like solar and wind are now the fastest-growing energy sectors worldwide.

How Can You Help?

  • Reduce, reuse, and recycle to minimize waste.
  • Use energy-efficient appliances and light bulbs.
  • Plant trees and support reforestation projects.
  • Switch to renewable energy sources like solar and wind.
  • Reduce car usage by walking, biking, or using public transport.

How Can You Help?

  • Adopt energy-efficient practices, such as using LED bulbs and energy-saving appliances.
  • Support renewable energy initiatives like solar, wind, and hydropower.
  • Reduce your carbon footprint by using public transportation, carpooling, or biking.
  • Plant trees and support reforestation projects to absorb CO2 from the atmosphere.
  • Advocate for policies that promote sustainability and reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Educate others about the importance of combating climate change and taking action.
